What is a TestOpsy?

A TestOpsy is a forensic examination of a software project's quality DNA.

Most organizations know they have quality problems. They see production defects, slow releases, unreliable test suites, flaky pipelines, and declining confidence in deployments. What they often don't know is why those problems exist, or where the next failure will occur.

A TestOpsy investigates the systems, processes, architecture, tooling, and feedback loops that shape software quality. Rather than focusing on individual defects, it identifies the underlying conditions that create them.

The Three Pillars of a TestOpsy

Failure Mode Analysis

We identify the technical and organizational conditions most likely to produce defects, regressions, delays, and operational risk.

Cause of Failure Investigation

We examine the factors driving quality issues, including architecture, testing strategy, release practices, team dynamics, and engineering workflows.

Quality Observability

We establish meaningful indicators of quality health so teams can move beyond intuition and monitor risk, confidence, and system reliability over time.

Deliverables

Every TestOpsy includes:

  • Current-state quality assessment
  • Risk and failure mode analysis
  • Findings report with prioritized recommendations
  • Executive summary
  • Follow-up review session

The goal isn't to find more bugs.

The goal is to understand why quality succeeds, why it fails, and what to do next.
